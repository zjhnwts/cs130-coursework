About ACALAB Summit

The Acalab Interdisciplinary Summit is a 7- to 10-day, non-profit summer academic research program organized by Acalab. During the activity, students will explore a cross-disciplinary topic in a laboratory environment with academic leaders, professors and tutors through literature collection, field experiments, and case studies. Each project will offer relevant courses and seminars, set up project-related Lab sessions and workshops, and invite well-known professors or industry practitioners in related fields to guide and share the project research. Acalab will also offer a core course, Research Design & Analysis, which includes Research methods, experimental Analysis, criticism of Research methods, academic writing, text reading, and presentation of results, with professional tutors with extensive experience in Research.

What are the Interdisciplinary Subjects?

The purpose of interdisciplinary research is to achieve integrated research on problems by transcending the previous research methods of categorization. The interdisciplinary mode of thinking is to satisfy the curiosity of exploring unknown fields, to ask the right questions in the face of difficult situations and point out multiple directions, and to stand shoulder to shoulder with collaborators in other fields and collided with the sparks of inspiration. The music theory which has been accumulated over thousands of years can also have the support of mathematical group theory; Complicated mathematical models in economics can also be interpreted by psychological cognitive factors and verified by experiments. The same social problem in cultural studies, anthropology, psychology, biology, applied mathematics will lead to different thinking and interpretation. At Acalab, you will meet like-minded partners, follow the torch of scientific truth, and use the cross-disciplinary thinking mode to recognize, question and change the world. To solve the great challenges facing society, scientists and industry must work together. Exploiting energy is not just about extracting resources where they are available. The layout of buildings requires knowledge of physical architecture, the impact on the local natural ecological environment requires knowledge of biology, and the political factors brought by it require knowledge of politicians and sociology. In solving social problems and academic problems, the research beyond the traditional academic boundaries is always the most comprehensive and effective.

The Importance of Independent Research

Independent research ability is an important part of the cultivation of academic ability and academic thinking. Researchers need to have the ability to choose problems, to solve problems independently, to explore unknown areas and design solutions, and to have ideological collisions with collaborators. However, the ability to do independent research is not limited to academic research. Every year, tens of thousands of doctors or research masters choose to work in industry or think tanks; Large enterprises set up research departments including mathematics, economics, cognitive science, data science, engineering and so on (R& D). Even in non-research-based work or daily life, we often have to consider the combination of different environmental, psychological, cultural and other factors. The problems revealed by a social phenomenon will involve the cross-interaction between individuals, groups and social backgrounds. Social phenomena reveal issues involving the interplay of individuals, groups, and social contexts that require consideration by research thinking and rigorous research methods or models.

What does an ideal interdisciplinary laboratory look like?

Founded in 2017, Acalab is a non-profit academic group dedicated to promoting education and research in the social sciences and cutting-edge interdisciplinary disciplines. Bringing together research practitioners from academia and industry, Acalab provides young students from around the world with the opportunity to learn and practice cutting-edge scientific exploration through its annual interdisciplinary science summits and research "incubation" program. ​

In the traditional single arrow teaching, the educated are more passive in learning knowledge and lack of active participation. In the future, however, there will be more emphasis on interactivity -- students will feel more involved and learn more efficiently. At present, interactive teaching mode is still limited to text and music; With the progress of science and technology, more diversified cognitive technologies will be applied to academic fields, such as touch, taste, and even smell.

With this in mind, Acalab wants to open up avenues of collaboration between disciplines, providing students with an open, interactive "lab" atmosphere. Mentors from different academic fields and industries bring more cutting-edge interdisciplinary science projects to students, combining theory with practice. Acalab will provide experimental funding and conference space for interactive presentations, creating an ideal social science and interdisciplinary laboratory. The lab is designed with a number of semi-open Spaces and meeting rooms with interactive screens. The contents of tablet computers and mobile phones can be projected on the screen anytime and anywhere, and the sparks of inspiration can be recorded on white boards and glass walls. From the moment students step into Acalab, they learn, research and interact with their peers and mentors in a relaxed and open environment. Acalab encourages students to take a holistic and inclusive view of the problem, to incorporate all aspects of the project into the scope of research and to explore more possible areas for the future.

With Acalab, you will:

  • Diversity

    The Acalab Interdisciplinary Summit offers multiple research projects for students to choose from. Each research project crosses the research barriers of a single discipline. The 2019 Summit projects include Linguistics and Philosophy, Experimental Economics and Computer Science, East Asian Studies and Sociology, Gender Studies and Cognitive Science, Bioinformatics, and Music Production and Engineering.

  • Cooperation between teachers and students

    We believe that successful research is based on successful collaboration. In the project, students not only learn about the academic background and mainstream research direction through class discussions, assignments, and question-answering under the leadership of the teaching assistant, but also explore the topic with researchers through seminars, in-depth reading of academic articles, and critical writing.

  • Research Methods

    During the summit, students will conduct independent research on one aspect of a cross-cutting topic under the guidance of academic leaders and professors. Due to the different emphases of different disciplines, the project research also covers research methods in multiple fields, including but not limited to text close reading, literature review, field experiments, case analysis, laboratory experiments, data statistics and analysis, model building, etc.

  • Presentation

    Academic presentation and presentation are indispensable skills in research. The students will present their research in a variety of ways at the Acalab Symposium on the final day of the summit. Excellent research results or experimental reports will be presented on multiple media platforms, and the academic team and professors will recommend relevant experimental research, academic conferences or social practice opportunities, and help students to contribute articles to academic journals and magazines in the field.
